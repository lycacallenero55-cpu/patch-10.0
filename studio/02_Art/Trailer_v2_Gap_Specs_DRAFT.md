### II.2 Design language — PROPOSAL "UNRENDERED CONTENT" (recommended), with named alternates

**Core idea (original to PATCH 10.0):** the word canon puts in quotes — "content" — is the design. These are not animals and not demons; they are **the Extinction Genre's spawned assets, shipped unfinished**. A monster reads as a flat ink-black creature-mass whose SILHOUETTE is confident but whose EDGES are still resolving: a narrow fringe of square pixel-static where the body meets the world, as if the renderer stopped caring. Inside the silhouette, almost nothing — flat black, cross-hatch texture at most — except **exactly one high-detail zone** (a mouth-cluster, an eye-row, a joint) rendered in crisp ink line, which is where the fear lives. Flat-2D readability comes from value mass + one detail zone, not from rendering (per Art_Direction's restraint doctrine: "real webtoon style is RESTRAINT," Appendix A).

- **Shape grammar (PROPOSAL):** wrong composites of familiar genre anatomy — a quadruped stance with one limb too many; a skull profile that quotes no real animal; proportions that feel "spawned to spec" rather than evolved. Rationale: 10.0 runs on dead worlds' corpses (canon above), so its content reads as carelessly recombined genre parts — but this stays a FLAVOR principle, not a lore claim ("monsters are literally built from dead worlds" is NOT asserted; that would be inventing cosmology — queued as a flavor-direction question, OQ-VIS-9).
- **Edge treatment (PROPOSAL):** square pixel-static fringe ONLY at silhouette boundary and wound/contact points — deliberately reusing the already-locked square-static vocabulary (sky-crack "square pixel-static debris," Art_Direction; FX-PATCH "square static flakes"; FX-ROLLBACK "square pixel-static flecks," Effects_Bible) so the monsters visibly belong to the patch's own visual system.
- **Moderator distinctness law (binding within this proposal):** `Kit_Specs_DRAFT.md` KIT-CHAR-006 already proposes square-static for the Moderator's face-redaction, so overlap must be managed, not discovered later: **Moderator = a HUMAN silhouette with its identity redacted (static replaces the face/interior; uniform intact; colorless; polite stillness; the world rolls back around it — Character_Bible CHAR-006). Monster = a NON-human creature-mass with static only at its edges; loud; mass-forward; it breaks things.** One is censorship wearing a person; the other is content without polish. If the Owner rejects static on monsters entirely to protect the Moderator's uniqueness, Alternate B below absorbs that ruling (OQ-VIS-9).
- **Color (PROPOSAL):** no glow, no accent, no eye-light by default — matte blacks/near-blacks only, so character accents light the scene (canon palette law above; also a deliberate divergence from the reference genre's glowing-eyed beasts, per the header's reference-library disclosure). Blood/ichor if ever shown: dark maroon only (Effects_Bible: "Blood: dark maroon spatter, never fountains").
- **Named alternates (offered, not recommended):** **(B) Pure ink-mass beasts** — same silhouette discipline, zero static, fear carried entirely by shape + the one detail zone (maximally safe for Moderator distinctness). **(C) Palimpsest-bodied** — visible strata of older-genre anatomy in the linework (a murim-beast shoulder, a mecha strut of a leg). Rejected as recommendation because it front-loads cosmology the story hasn't shown and risks reading as busy against the flat-2D restraint law — but listed so the Owner sees the fork (OQ-VIS-9).

### III.2 Originality clause — what the genre reference showed, and what we therefore do NOT do

Per the OPS-0002 handoff, the PMU library's card-UI refs demonstrate the GENRE's grammar (PMU-086-D, PMU-142-D, PMU-144-D viewed, hash-verified against INDEX §A): gacha-style portrait cards with ornate filigree borders, rarity stars, bottom name-plates, red status ribbons — diagonal in some chapters, banded in others — reading "BLEEDING"/"KILLED IN ACTION," arranged in party grids under calligraphic headers; plus an ornate dark infographic-plaque variant for system exposition. **That grammar is the sensibility datum, not the design.** PATCH 10.0's registry card uses: no gacha framing, no rarity stars, no gilded ornament, no diagonal ribbons, no party-grid presentation, no collectible-card aspect ratio as identity. Our device is not a game inventory — it is **a school's paper roster being overwritten by a changelog.**

### III.3 Design — PROPOSAL "LEDGER UNDER REVISION" (recommended)

**Concept (original):** each card is a **Soryeong Academy student roster card** — a warm, humane, paper object from the 9.0 world — being **overwritten live by the system's cyan-on-black plaque layer**, and then **stamped**. The horror is the interface: the world's paperwork is only a skin over the changelog. One card = one student = one revision event.

- **Substrate & layout (PROPOSAL):** vertical card, paper-white/warm parchment field; flat-2D linework per style law. Top: academy crest line + "SORYEONG ACADEMY — STUDENT REGISTRY" register line (small, formal). Middle-left: student portrait as a flat-2D ink bust in a plain rectangular frame (no ornament). Middle-right/below: ruled ledger fields in handwriting-style entries (name, year, discipline, practical record) — the forged-paper warmth cited above. Bottom: the academy's own stamp/seal box (the mundane bureaucracy that the system's stamp will rhyme against).
- **The overwrite layer (PROPOSAL):** from one edge (matching the wave's diagonal grammar, 20–25° family), the paper DRAINS — color desaturates and the ruled lines re-render as thin cyan-white vector rules on near-black (FX-HUD kinship), the handwritten fields re-setting as system text inside 【 】 brackets; a faint dead-script glyph row (Art_Direction's sky-crack "faint alien glyph row" vocabulary) runs where the paper's watermark was — illegible by design, baked into the art. Square pixel-static at the conversion boundary. The card is thus BOTH canon UI registers at once — 9.0 paper and system plaque — with the patch caught mid-crossing.
- **The stamp (PROPOSAL):** a harsh rectangular seal slamming over the portrait/name: **REWRITTEN** — clinical, not gory. Recommended rendering: system-cyan-white hot core with static-fleck edge bleed (system color law; "system = clinical tones" argues against blood-red; PMU's red ribbons are also specifically what we're designing away from). Bracket treatment 【REWRITTEN】offered as an option. Stamp color/bracket choice queued (OQ-VIS-11).
- **Typography & hierarchy (PROPOSAL):** three tiers — (1) the stamp: largest, instant read (this is the 2.5s cold-open's entire job); (2) student name field: second read; (3) everything else: texture, deliberately sub-legible at vertical-video scale. No locked wordmark/typeface exists in the repo (KF plan KF-16 row records the same gap for the logo); interim recommendation: the existing Pillow-recipe face (DejaVuSans-Bold, `Video_Workflow.md` appendix) for the stamp layer, handwriting-style linework (drawn, not fonted) for ledger fields. Typeface decision queued with OQ-VIS-11.

The STAMP and any legible status text are applied as overlay layers (Pillow/HTML) per the lettering law ("HTML/Pillow only — never baked," Art_Direction.md Lettering) — which also gives assembly exact control of the ×3 stamp timing against drum hits, and lets the same base card art serve REWRITTEN / DEPRECATED / future states without regeneration.
